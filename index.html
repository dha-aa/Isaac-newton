Isaac Newton

Sir Isaac Newton (1643–1727) was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, and author. Widely recognized as one of the most influential scientists of all time, Newton formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation.

Key Achievements

  • 1687: Published Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica, laying the groundwork for classical mechanics.
  • 1666: Discovered the law of universal gravitation while observing an apple fall.
  • 1665–1666: Developed calculus (independently of Leibniz).
  • 1672: Elected Fellow of the Royal Society.
  • 1703: Became President of the Royal Society.
